# Cursory Services — Overview

## What Cursory Services are

Cursory Services are human-delivered AI governance advisory and implementation work. They are not platform features. They run because a consultant runs them. The regulatory mapping, the risk framework, the policy design, and the control testing are Cursory's intellectual property. None of it is provided by the Ethana platform.

This is the layer the earlier files incorrectly attributed to the Ethana product. AI risk assessments, gap analyses, framework mapping delivery, control implementation guidance, and regulatory watch are all Services, not platform capabilities.

## The relationship to the platform

Cursory is not a software reseller. A reseller makes 15 to 25 percent margin and is replaced when the vendor goes direct. Cursory's defensible position is an AI governance advisory practice that uses Ethana as its technical delivery platform.

The client buys AI governance outcomes from Cursory. Ethana is the infrastructure that delivers part of those outcomes. The motion is: enter through advisory (the assessment that reveals the gap), land on software (Ethana as the control layer), expand through managed services (the ongoing retainer).

## The separation rule

When positioning, keep the line clean:

- If it runs without a person present, it is the Ethana platform. Classify it Production / In Build / Roadmap.
- If a person delivers it, it is a Cursory Service. It does not get a platform status flag, because it is available the day Cursory has the capacity to deliver it.

Do not present a Service as a product feature. A buyer who later discovers that "framework mapping" was a consulting engagement, not software, will distrust every other claim.

## Where Services and platform overlap

Several Services use platform capabilities as their delivery mechanism. Examples:

- Red Teaming as a Service uses the platform's red-teaming orchestrator.
- Ethana Implementation configures the platform (gateway, guardrail policy, audit schema, SIEM integration).
- Managed Ethana Operations runs the platform on the client's behalf.

In these cases, state both halves plainly: the platform capability (status-flagged) and the human delivery wrapped around it.

## Where a Service substitutes for an In Build platform capability

Some Services exist today precisely because the equivalent platform capability is In Build or Roadmap. This is a feature of the model, not a problem, as long as it is stated honestly.

- Framework Mapping is delivered by Cursory consultants today. The platform's Compliance Pack (automated evidence collectors and one-click export) is In Build. When it ships, it will partly automate this Service.
- AI Inventory and Classification is delivered as a Cursory engagement today. The platform's Discovery capability is Roadmap. When it ships, it will partly automate this Service.

Position the Service as the way to get the outcome now, and the platform capability as the future automation, without implying the platform already does it.
